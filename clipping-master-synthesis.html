Real Risks — Eyes Open

⚠ Platform shadowbanning: Algorithms punish spam. Accounts that mass-post identical clips across multiple accounts without warming up first get suppressed. Warm up every new account with 3–5 organic posts before mass scheduling.
⚠ Campaign fraud: Some campaigns are scammy. Some clippers bot their views — and Whop's anti-bot detection doesn't catch 100% of it. Stick to campaigns from verifiable creators. Check "most paid out" sort to confirm payment history.
⚠ Copyright risk: Clipping without authorization is grey-area fair use. Always clip within official Whop/Vyro campaign guidelines where the creator has explicitly authorized it. Adding captions, overlays, or trending audio also helps fair use claims.
⚠ Budget timing: Campaigns drain. If a campaign is 90%+ consumed, you risk posting great content and earning nothing. Only enter at 60% or more remaining budget. Fast clippers who post within 24hrs of campaign launch get the most payout runway.
⚠ Saturation is real and rising: The NPR article signals mainstream awareness. The "12–18 month window" estimate is the consensus across all sources. Early movers win. Waiters compete against a much larger field for compressed payouts.
⚠ Platform CPM compression: Per-clip payouts may drop as more clippers flood the market. The counter-strategy is building owned audience assets (email list, subscription) alongside clip CPM — so you're not entirely dependent on marketplace rates.
